Who We Are

Our Story

A cup of coffee, a family recipe, and a dream to share it with the world.

How It Started

From Lola's Kitchen
to Your Cup

It started in a small kitchen in Sagada, where our grandmother Carmen would wake before dawn to brew barako beans in a clay pot worn smooth from decades of use. No electric grinders, no pressure gauges — just fire, time, and an instinct for flavor passed down through generations of mountain farmers.

Her recipe traveled south when the family moved to Puerto Galera, tucked into a worn notebook alongside photographs and handwritten notes in Ilocano. What began as weekend gatherings and cups shared with neighbors slowly grew into something bigger: a vision for a cafe that honored those roots while welcoming everyone to the table.

Today, Kape Ni Lola is that vision made real. We brew specialty Filipino coffee with care and craft, but we never forget that it all started with Lola Carmen, a clay pot, and the simple belief that a good cup of coffee can make a stranger feel like family.

Our Journey

Milestones That Matter

2018
The First Cup

Started as a small kiosk at Tabinay market on weekends. A folding table, a hand-grinder, and Lola's barako. Word spread faster than we expected.

2019
A Permanent Home

Opened our first sit-down cafe space on Tabinay Road — eight tables, hand-painted walls, and a proper espresso setup. The regulars finally had a place to linger.

2020
Staying Strong

Pivoted to takeout and delivery during lockdowns. We launched coffee kits and bean subscriptions to keep our community caffeinated — and our staff employed — through a difficult year.

2022
Growing the Family

Expanded our seating to 30 covers, added a full kitchen, and launched our food menu featuring Lola's ensaymada and leche flan recipes alongside our coffee program.

2024
Featured & Recognized

Named one of Mindoro's top cafes by the local tourism board. A quiet honor that reminded us why we started: to put Puerto Galera's coffee culture on the map.

What We Stand For

Our Core Values

🌱
Locally Sourced

We source 100% of our beans from Benguet and Sagada farmers. Farm-to-cup, always. Every bag tells you exactly where it came from and who grew it.

🤝
Community First

We employ locals, support nearby farms, and give back to the barangay that raised us. Our success only means something if it lifts the people around us.

☕
Quality Above All

Every drink is made to order. No shortcuts, no compromises, just craft. From the grind setting to the water temperature — every variable is intentional.

🌿
Sustainability

Eco-friendly packaging, zero single-use plastics, and composting from day one. We treat the environment the way Lola treated her garden — with respect and care.

🫶
Warmth & Welcome

We treat every customer like they're sitting at Lola's dining table. Whether it's your first visit or your five hundredth, you belong here.

📚
Education

We run monthly barista workshops and coffee appreciation sessions for locals. Knowledge should be as freely shared as a good cup of kape.

Our Sourcing Philosophy

Direct Trade, Real Relationships

We don't buy through middlemen. Every batch of beans comes directly from farming families we've visited, eaten with, and built trust with over years. That relationship means better prices for farmers and better beans for you.

Our head barista Maria visits both regions at least twice a year — during harvest and post-processing — to work alongside the farmers, taste the lots, and ensure the quality that makes it onto our menu is something everyone in the chain is proud of.

When you order a cup at Kape Ni Lola, you're not just tasting coffee. You're tasting the altitude, the rainfall, the hands of the people who grew it, and the story of a grandmother who believed great kape deserved to be shared.
